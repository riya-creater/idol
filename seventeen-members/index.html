Struggles and Success Phases:

Struggles: Before debuting, SEVENTEEN faced the pressures of a long training period, especially since some members had been trainees for several years. Financial difficulties at Pledis Entertainment also posed a challenge, and there was uncertainty about whether the group would even debut. Additionally, being a 13-member group meant they had to work hard to synchronize their performances and build a cohesive team.

Success: SEVENTEEN‚Äôs first major success came with 17 Carat, which solidified their place as a rising K-pop group. Over time, their self-producing image helped them stand out in a crowded industry. As they released hit albums like Love & Letter, Al1, and An Ode, their popularity skyrocketed globally.

Their successful “Diamond Edge” and “Ode to You” world tours showcased their growth, and SEVENTEEN became known for their synchronization and live performances. In 2020, with the release of their EP Heng, they surpassed one million album sales in South Korea, achieving a significant milestone in their career.

SEVENTEEN’s influence has extended beyond music into fashion, philanthropy, and even environmental initiatives, marking their place as one of the most well-rounded groups in K-pop today. Their success continues to grow with each new release, supported by their dedicated Carat fandom.
